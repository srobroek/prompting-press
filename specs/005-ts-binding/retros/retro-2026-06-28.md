## ✅ What Went Well (keep doing)

- **The kernel/consumer split paid off a second time.** 005 is the *second* FFI binding — render parity
  is now demonstrated *empirically* across two independent bindings: TS and Python emit byte-identical
  `templateHash`/`renderHash` for the same prompt+inputs. That's the Principle I guarantee the whole
  architecture exists for, and it's exactly what spec 006's conformance corpus will lock down.
- **004's hard-won lessons transferred and saved real time.** The 004 critique's two Must-Address traps
  (kernel-direct render; SEC-004 scrub on the kernel path) were baked into the 005 plan *up front* and
  re-verified against source — so 005's critique was PROCEED, not PROCEED-WITH-UPDATES. The CI-gate
  lessons (I1: wire binding tests into CI; the maturin/libpython link bug) became `ci:test-node` from
  the start.
- **The review genuinely improved the product.** review.run found a real cross-binding **parity gap**
  (TS `render` couldn't select a variant), which escalated into a **codebase-wide convention**
  (options objects, constitution v1.1.0 / C-11) that improved all three bindings — not just a local fix.
- **Integrity-check protocol + main-thread re-verification worked.** Every audit/review subagent that
  ran got the integrity-check preamble; the ones with a working channel were accurate and I re-verified
  their load-bearing findings against source. No fabricated finding made it into a decision this cycle.
- **The 3 parallel idiom audits (refactoring.guru + style guides) were high-value** and consistent —
  they confirmed quality, caught the `--prod` comment drift, the row-shape gap, and the py.typed gap.

## ⚠️ What Could Improve (start/stop)

- **An in-cycle constitution amendment creates a doc-drift blast radius.** Adopting C-11 mid-review meant
  the 005 spec/contract AND the *merged* 004 quickstart went stale against the code in the same cycle.
  Both were caught by sync (15/16) and fixed — but the lesson: **when an amendment lands mid-cycle, run a
  doc-reconcile sweep immediately**, not just at the sync gate. **Start:** treat "amendment → grep every
  spec/contract/quickstart/README for the old shape" as part of the amendment checklist.
- **The spec was written before the convention existed**, so FR-009/FR-012 described the pre-C-11 shape.
  Unavoidable for a convention *discovered* in review, but it argues for surfacing call-shape decisions
  at `/speckit.plan` (the contract phase), where they're cheap, not at review.
- **`render`'s `isSchema` duck-typing survived** (accepted) — the one spot the options-object refactor
  didn't dissolve, because `render`'s schema+data are required-positional. A future reader may re-flag it;
  it's documented as accepted, but a fully-object `render({schema,data,...})` would have been *uniformly*
  clean. Left as-is per the "required operands stay positional" rule.
